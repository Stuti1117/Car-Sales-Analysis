# Car-Sales-Analysis
# 📊 Sales Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an **interactive Sales Analysis Dashboard** built using Power BI.
It provides deep insights into **sales performance, profit margins, product trends, and customer distribution**.

The dashboard helps businesses understand **what drives sales and profitability**.

---

## 📊 Dashboard Highlights

### 🔹 Key KPIs

* 💰 Total Sales: **$9.6M**
* 📈 Profit Margin %: **39.84%**
* 💵 Total Profit: **$3.83M**
* 🚗 Top Selling Product: **1992 Ferrari 360 Spider Red**
* 📅 Monthly Sales Growth: **+54.97%**

---

## 📷 Dashboard Features

### 📦 Product Line Analysis

* Sales distribution across product categories:

  * Classic Cars
  * Vintage Cars
  * Motorcycles
  * Trucks & Buses
  * Planes, Ships, Trains
* Classic Cars generate the highest revenue

---

### 📊 Sales Trend Analysis

* Year-wise sales comparison (2003–2005)
* Peak sales observed in **2004**
* Decline trend in 2005

---

### 🛍 Top & Bottom Products

* Top 5 best-selling products identified
* Bottom 5 low-performing products highlighted
* Helps in inventory and marketing decisions

---

### 🌍 Customer Distribution

* Map visualization showing global customer locations
* High customer concentration in **Europe and North America**

---

### 📈 Quantity Analysis

* Quantity ordered by:

  * Year
  * Product line
* Helps track demand patterns

---

### 🔍 Key Influencers Analysis

* Identifies factors that **increase sales**
* Example:

  * Product features like detailed interior & engine increase sales
* Useful for business decision-making

---

### ⏱ Time Series Analysis

* Daily sales trend using order date
* Shows fluctuations and seasonal patterns

---

## 🎯 Key Features

* Interactive filters (Year, City, Product Line, Employee)

## 📈 Insights Derived

* 📌 Classic Cars contribute the highest revenue
* 📌 2004 was the best-performing sales year
* 📌 Certain product features significantly boost sales
* 📌 Sales are concentrated in developed regions
* 📌 A few products generate majority of revenue (Pareto effect)
